# Bitcoin and Privacy Hackathon Official Rules

NO PURCHASE OR PAYMENT NECESSARY TO ENTER OR WIN.

SUBMISSION OF ANY ENTRY CONSTITUTES AGREEMENT TO THESE OFFICIAL RULES AS A CONTRACT BETWEEN ENTRANT, HACKATHON SPONSOR, AND DORAHACKS.

1. Dates
   Registration Opens: Wednesday, January 15, 2026 Hacking Period: Sunday, February 1 – Saturday, February 28, 2026, 23:59 UTC Registration Closes: With submissions — Saturday, February 28, 2026, 23:59 UTC Judging Period: Sunday, March 1 – Friday, March 13, 2026 Winners Announced: Saturday, March 15, 2026

2. Eligibility

#### Open to:

Individuals who are at least the age of majority where they reside
Teams of eligible individuals
Legally established organizations
If a Team or Organization enters, they must designate an authorized Representative to act on their behalf.

#### Not open to:

Entities involved in the design, production, or distribution of the Hackathon
Employees and immediate family members of such entities
Judges or companies employing a Judge
Anyone whose participation would create a conflict of interest

3. How to Enter
   Register on DoraHacks with a new or existing account
   Develop a Project that meets the requirements
   Complete all fields on the Submission page

### Project Requirements

Tracks:

- Bitcoin: Bitcoin integration with Starknet (bridges, atomic swaps, BTCFi, trust-minimized infrastructure)
- Privacy: Privacy-preserving technology (encrypted compute, ZK apps, private transactions, identity)
- Open Innovation: Any innovative project on Starknet

Conditions:

- Must be functional and run on the intended platform
- New projects or existing ones with significant updates during the hackathon

### Submission Requirements

- Project description
- Demo video (3 minutes max)
- Functional demo URL
- Public code repository
- README included
- Starknet wallet address
- Optional: Pitch deck

### Track Eligibility Rule

Projects must genuinely fit the track they're submitted to. Submissions that don't meet the track criteria will be disqualified.

Examples:

- Privacy Track: Must use privacy-preserving tech (ZK proofs, confidential transactions, Tongo, Garaga, etc.). A project that doesn't implement any privacy features will be disqualified.

- Bitcoin Track: Must integrate Bitcoin (BTC wrappers, bridges, BTCFi). A project with no Bitcoin component will be disqualified.

### Language

Materials in English or with English translation.

4. Seed Grantee Eligibility
   If you're a current Starknet Foundation seed grantee, you can participate in the hackathon with:

- A completely new project, OR
- A clear extension of your current funded project

5. Judging
   Stage One: Verify basic viability and alignment with theme.

Stage Two: Equally weighted criteria:

- Technological Execution: Is it well built?
- Innovation: Is it original and creative?
- Impact: Does it solve a real problem?
- Presentation: Is it clearly explained?
- Progress: How much was achieved during the hackathon?
  Tie Breaking: Highest score on first criterion; if tied, next criterion.
